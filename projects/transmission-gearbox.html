Transmission Gearbox

The Transmission Gearbox project focused on designing an efficient and durable gearbox system for automotive applications, optimizing torque distribution and energy transmission for enhanced vehicle performance.

Role

Lead Engineer & Designer

Date

May - June 2024

Tools

SolidWorks, MATLAB, Python, Lathe, Mill

Skills

Mechanical Engineering, Product Design, System Optimization, GD&T, CAD Modeling, Team Leadership, Product Development, Manufacturing Techniques, Team Collaboration

Overview

This project focused on the design and optimization of a spur gear transmission system intended for automotive applications, aiming to enhance power transmission efficiency, reduce friction, and improve overall system reliability. The gearbox is custom-designed with a 6:1 gear ratio, making it ideal for applications in traditional and electric vehicles.

Problem Statement


The goal was to create a gearbox that maximizes energy transfer efficiency while maintaining durability and low operating costs. This system had to meet tight dimensional constraints and integrate seamlessly with existing powertrains.

Solutions


  • Designed a reverted spur gear train with a 6:1 gear ratio, using four spur gears to minimize friction and optimize torque transfer.
  • Compact dimensions: 5.5 x 4 x 6 in³, ensuring integration within tight automotive space constraints.
  • Material selection: Carbon steel for gears (durability, wear resistance) and acrylic for structural components (cost-efficiency).
  • Designed and manufactured the majority of the gearbox components using milling and lathe operations.
  • Applied Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) to ensure precise and consistent manufacturing tolerances.
  • Created a 3D CAD model of the gearbox using SolidWorks to visualize and optimize the design before manufacturing.

Impact


  • Optimized gear ratio and design choices resulted in enhanced transmission efficiency, achieving the desired peak speed.
  • The fabricated gearbox demonstrated robustness and reliability during testing, ensuring long-term performance.

  1. Design Concept & Refinement: Initial designs included four spur gears, each with a 2.45:1 ratio, achieving an overall 6:1 ratio. Dimensions and materials were selected to balance cost, strength, and wear resistance.

  2. Gear & Shaft Selection:
    • Gear Selection: 15-tooth and 36-tooth Carbon steel gears designed for strength and efficiency.
    • Shaft Selection: Aluminum D-profile shafts with collars to minimize play and ensure smooth operation.
  3. Simulation & Testing:
    • Modeled the design in SolidWorks
    • Prototype tested with acrylic supports and carbon steel gears, confirming smooth torque transfer and minimal friction.


  4. Advanced Manufacturing Techniques:
    • Applied Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) to ensure precise and consistent manufacturing tolerances.
    • Fabricated the majority of the gearbox components using milling and lathe operations

Process & Methodology

Final Product

Designed and fabricated an efficient mechanical transmission system for a DC motor-powered bicycle wheel, achieving a peak speed of 512 RPM to ensure reliable power transmission. The gear ratio and design were optimized through detailed CAD modeling and calculations, balancing performance with durability.

Takeaways & Learnings



Material Selection

Carefully selecting materials for performance, durability, and cost-efficiency is crucial in mechanical design.

Testing and Validation

Thorough testing and validation are necessary to ensure that the gearbox functions optimally under real-world conditions.

Design Optimization

Optimizing design elements, such as gear ratio and component sizing, is key to maximizing efficiency and longevity.

Collaboration

Working within a multidisciplinary team allowed for diverse perspectives and a more robust final product.

Process Iteration

Iterative design and constant prototyping were essential for refining the gearbox’s performance and ensuring its efficiency.
