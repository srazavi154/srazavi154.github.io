Robotic Mirror Therapy Exoskeleton

Accessibility-Focused Biomedical Design | 2024

The Healthcare Gap

[cite_start]

With commercial exoskeletons costing between $75,000 and $98,000, children with hemiplegic cerebral palsy often lack access to critical rehabilitation technology[cite: 216, 218]. [cite_start]This project achieved a 99.5% cost reduction while maintaining therapeutic efficacy[cite: 213].

Design Innovation

[cite_start]

The system uses a Master-Slave Architecture where a non-motorized arm driven by the patient's functional hand provides position data to a motorized slave arm that mimics the movement on the impaired limb[cite: 231, 232, 233].

    [cite_start]
  • Hardware: Powered by a single ATmega328p Arduino using bi-directional potentiometer sensing[cite: 234, 235].
    • [cite_start]
  • Control Algorithm: Implements an exponential slowdown when approaching target positions to prevent overshooting[cite: 244].
    • [cite_start]
  • Safety: Integrated overcurrent protection and position limit enforcement to prevent hyperextension[cite: 246].

Manufacturing Strategy

[cite_start]

The final cost of **$34.28 per unit** was achieved by substituting machined metal with 3D-printed plastic components and utilizing off-the-shelf motors and drivers[cite: 246].
