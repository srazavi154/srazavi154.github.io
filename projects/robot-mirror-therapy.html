Robotic Mirror Therapy Exoskeleton

Accessibility-Focused Biomedical Design | 2022

The Healthcare Gap

With commercial exoskeletons costing between $75,000 and $98,000, children with hemiplegic cerebral palsy often lack access to critical rehabilitation technology. This project achieved a 99.5% cost reduction while maintaining therapeutic efficacy.

Design Innovation

The system uses a Master-Slave Architecture where a non-motorized arm driven by the patient's functional hand provides position data to a motorized slave arm that mimics the movement on the impaired limb.

  • Hardware: Powered by a single ATmega328p Arduino using bi-directional potentiometer sensing.
  • Control Algorithm: Implements an exponential slowdown when approaching target positions to prevent overshooting.
  • Safety: Integrated overcurrent protection and position limit enforcement to prevent hyperextension.

Manufacturing Strategy

The final cost of **$34.28 per unit** was achieved by substituting machined metal with 3D-printed plastic components and utilizing off-the-shelf motors and drivers.
