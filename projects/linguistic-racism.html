Linguistic Racism & Anxiety

Physiological Computing & Bias Detection | 2022

The Research Motivation

Linguistic racism—prejudice based on accents or language proficiency—has severe mental health impacts, yet quantitative research remains limited. This study provides objective evidence of anxiety-like symptoms triggered by linguistic bias.

Quantification Methodology

Using wearable technology, we captured real-time, moment-by-moment emotional responses to linguistic racism content.

  • Technology: Utilized the Empatica E4 for heart rate (PPG), electrodermal activity (EDA), and temperature, alongside a Muse Brain Sensing Headband for EEG data.
  • Markers: Monitored for decreased Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and increased EDA peak frequency, which are physiological indicators of stress and arousal.

Broader Implications

This data serves as objective evidence for academic policy changes and the development of real-time support systems for vulnerable populations.

Tech Stack

  • Empatica E4
  • Muse EEG
  • Python (Data Analysis)

Design

Controlled study with 10 participants (NES vs NNES).
