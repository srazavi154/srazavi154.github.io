Linguistic Racism & Anxiety

Physiological Computing & Bias Detection | 2024

The Research Motivation

[cite_start]

Linguistic racism—prejudice based on accents or language proficiency—has severe mental health impacts, yet quantitative research remains limited[cite: 259]. [cite_start]This study provides objective evidence of anxiety-like symptoms triggered by linguistic bias[cite: 258].

Quantification Methodology

[cite_start]

Using wearable technology, we captured real-time, moment-by-moment emotional responses to linguistic racism content[cite: 261].

    [cite_start]
  • Technology: Utilized the Empatica E4 for heart rate (PPG), electrodermal activity (EDA), and temperature, alongside a Muse Brain Sensing Headband for EEG data[cite: 262, 263].
    • [cite_start]
  • Markers: Monitored for decreased Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and increased EDA peak frequency, which are physiological indicators of stress and arousal[cite: 264].

Broader Implications

[cite_start]

This data serves as objective evidence for academic policy changes and the development of real-time support systems for vulnerable populations[cite: 265, 266].

Tech Stack

  • Muse EEG
  • Python (Data Analysis)

Design

[cite_start]

Controlled study with 10 participants (NES vs NNES)[cite: 261].
