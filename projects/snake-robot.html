Biologically Inspired Snake Robot

The Snake Robot is a biologically inspired robotic system designed for seamless navigation and obstacle avoidance, mimicking the fluid movement of real snakes.

Role

Lead Designer & Developer

Date

Nov - Dec 2024

Tools

MATLAB, Python, ROS2, URDF, Gazebo

Skills

Robotics Engineering, Path Planning, Control Systems, Obstacle Avoidance, Simulation, Product Development, Team Collaboration

Overview

The snake robot project focused on developing a biologically inspired robotic system capable of lateral undulation to navigate complex, confined environments. By simulating natural snake movement, we addressed real-world problems, such as improving the adaptability and flexibility of robots in dynamic environments—critical for industries such as search-and-rescue, exploration, and medical applications.

Problem Statement


Traditional robots struggle with navigating through narrow or obstructed spaces. This project sought to develop a robot that mimicked snake-like movements to overcome these challenges, creating a versatile and efficient solution for real-world applications.

Solutions


  • Mimic Snake Movement: Efficient, smooth, and precise motion tailored to navigate tight spaces.
  • Advanced Obstacle Avoidance: Integrated real-time sensor feedback for dynamic path adjustments in cluttered environments.
  • Modular Design for Scalability: The robot's 16-DOF arm structure allows for adaptability in various applications, from industrial to medical scenarios.

Impact


  • Use Cases: Disaster relief, confined-space inspections, medical applications (e.g., snake-like surgery aids), and autonomous exploration.
  • Technological Advancements: Achieved near-real-time response times and seamless integration with robotic systems via ROS2, showcasing my expertise in advanced robotics control systems.

  1. User & Market Research: Through extensive research, we identified the need for adaptable robots in challenging environments. Key insights included:
    • Studying snake movement dynamics to design efficient locomotion algorithms.
    • Understanding user needs in robotics and rescue operations, focusing on navigating confined spaces.
  2. Ideation & Technical Planning: We developed key features like lateral undulation path planning algorithms, real-time obstacle avoidance using machine learning, and a modular 16-DOF robot design.
  3. Competitive Edge: Our system stands out for its efficiency in real-time performance and scalability for various applications.
  4. Product Development & Iteration: We developed prototypes, refined algorithms, and tested extensively in virtual environments before real-world deployment.

Process & Methodology

Final Product

The final product demonstrated several key features: dynamic navigation with smooth and adaptive movement using lateral undulation, enabling the robot to navigate through complex environments; obstacle avoidance through real-time feedback systems, allowing the robot to modify its path and avoid potential collisions; and a scalable architecture, ready for integration with additional sensors and hardware, ensuring the robot can adapt to and tackle real-world challenges.

Takeaways & Learnings



Innovation through Collaboration

A truly successful robotics product requires close collaboration with various stakeholders, including engineers, designers, and end-users.

Continuous Iteration

Rapid prototyping, rigorous testing, and constant iteration were key to refining the robot’s performance and achieving a competitive final product.

Efficient Product Design

Learning to balance technical feasibility with user needs was crucial in creating a product that is both functional and viable in real-world applications.

Importance of Simulation for Testing

Simulating complex environments before real-world deployment ensured we could identify and solve potential problems early, saving time and resources.

User-Centered Design Approach

User feedback during development played a crucial role in refining the system. Understanding how end-users interact with the robot helped shape features that directly met their needs.
