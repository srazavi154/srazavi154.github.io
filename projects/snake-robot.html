Locomotion Architecture

[cite_start]

This project replicated lateral undulation observed in biological snakes through a system of 16 degrees of freedom (8 pairs of joints)[cite: 95, 97].

Kinematic Control

    [cite_start]
  • Undulation: Joint angles were calculated using $\theta_{i}(t)=A\cdot sin(2\pi ft+\varphi_{i})$ to create a wave propagation effect[cite: 100, 103].
    • [cite_start]
  • Secondary Task: Implemented real-time obstacle avoidance via repulsive force vectors ($F_{repulsion} \propto 1/distance$)[cite: 106, 108].
    • [cite_start]
  • Null-Space Projection: Primary locomotion and secondary avoidance were combined using null-space projection to prevent motion conflicts[cite: 113, 130].

Stability Monitoring

[cite_start]

To ensure smooth motion, I implemented real-time monitoring of the Jacobian condition number[cite: 116]. [cite_start]The periodic oscillation of this number indicated stable kinematic configurations throughout the motion cycle[cite: 121, 122].

[cite_start]

Result: Achieved smooth, biologically-realistic movement with controlled velocity magnitudes ($\pm0.04$ rad/s) and no observed instabilities[cite: 124, 125].
