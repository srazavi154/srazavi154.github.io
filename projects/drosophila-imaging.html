Calcium Imaging of Drosophila Olfaction

Neural Encoding & Signal Processing | 2024

Research Objective

[cite_start]

This study investigated the fundamental principles of olfactory coding by visualizing neural activity in Drosophila melanogaster[cite: 249]. [cite_start]We revealed that odor identity is encoded through unique combinations of excitatory and inhibitory responses rather than a simple binary pattern[cite: 249].

Technical Pipeline

[cite_start]

We used GCaMP6f, a genetically encoded calcium indicator where fluorescence intensity is proportional to neural activity[cite: 250].

    [cite_start]
  • Data Processing: Calculated $\Delta F/F$ to normalize baseline fluorescence variations and enable comparison across regions of interest (ROIs)[cite: 252].
    • [cite_start]
  • Heatmap Generation: Visualized fluorescence changes during odor delivery to identify combinatorial coding patterns[cite: 253].
    • [cite_start]
  • Insights: Discovered that attractive odors can have inhibitory components, while aversive odors can have excitatory ones[cite: 254, 255].
