Calcium Imaging of Drosophila Olfaction

Neural Encoding & Signal Processing | 2025

Research Objective

This study investigated the fundamental principles of olfactory coding by visualizing neural activity in Drosophila melanogaster. We revealed that odor identity is encoded through unique combinations of excitatory and inhibitory responses rather than a simple binary pattern.

Technical Pipeline

We used GCaMP6f, a genetically encoded calcium indicator where fluorescence intensity is proportional to neural activity.

  • Data Processing: Calculated $\Delta F/F$ to normalize baseline fluorescence variations and enable comparison across regions of interest (ROIs).
  • Heatmap Generation: Visualized fluorescence changes during odor delivery to identify combinatorial coding patterns.
  • Insights: Discovered that attractive odors can have inhibitory components, while aversive odors can have excitatory ones.
